## Option A: "Factory First" (Conservative)

### Overview
Complete the AI Factory automation before building more products. Focus on improving the machine rather than using it. Strengthen the foundation before scaling output.

### Pros
- Reduces technical debt before it compounds
- Ensures repeatable, high-quality output
- Builds true moat around the factory system
- Maintains quality standards (SOTA requirement)
- Aligns with "Factory First" stated priority

### Cons
- **Delays revenue indefinitely** (already pre-revenue)
- Risk of over-engineering without real-world validation
- Opportunity cost while market moves
- Psychological toll of building without shipping
- Emotional thesis remains untested in market

### Effort & Risk
| Dimension | Rating | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Effort | High | Systems work, not product work |
| Risk | Medium | Safe technically, risky business-wise |
| Viability | 65% | Solid engineering, questionable timing |

### Key Dependencies
- Clear definition of "factory complete"
- Test requirements for each automation
- Validation that automation actually saves time
- Commitment to ship after factory improvements

---

## Option B: "Ship to Learn" (Balanced, Recommended)

### Overview
**Activate the factory NOW.** Ship the 3 pending MVPs with current automation, learn from real deployments, improve factory based on actual usage patterns. The "Toyota Production System" approach - optimize what you actually use.

### Pros
- **Fastest path to revenue validation**
- Real feedback loops guide factory improvements
- Tests emotional thesis in actual market
- Breaks analysis paralysis
- Builds momentum and learning
- Factory improvements targeted at real friction points

### Cons
- May ship with imperfect automation
- Some manual work required initially
- Potential for rework as factory improves
- Quality gate pressure with faster shipping

### Effort & Risk
| Dimension | Rating | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Effort | Medium | Ship first, optimize second |
| Risk | Medium | Execution risk, but fast learning |
| Viability | 82% | Balanced approach with feedback loops |

### Key Dependencies
- Activate existing factory (minimal setup)
- Quality gates must still pass (SOTA)
- Commitment to iterate based on learnings
- Acceptance of "good enough" initial automation

### Core Philosophy
> "The factory doesn't need to be perfect to ship. It needs to be good enough to learn."

## Option C: "Portfolio Blitz" (Aggressive)

### Overview
Ship all 3 pending MVPs in parallel using full parallelization (10 haiku workers). Maximum velocity, maximum learning. Let the market decide while improving factory in real-time.

### Pros
- **Maximum learning speed** - 3 parallel experiments
- Highest probability of finding winner
- Tests the full portfolio thesis simultaneously
- Forces prioritization and ruthless scoping
- Validates emotional thesis across 3 different markets

### Cons
- **Highest execution risk** - 3x complexity
- Quality assurance challenges
- Founder bandwidth spread thin
- No time for factory improvements between ships
- Risk of 3 mediocre products instead of 1 great one

### Effort & Risk
| Dimension | Rating | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Effort | High | 3x parallel development |
| Risk | High | Execution complexity |
| Viability | 68% | High risk, high reward |

### Key Dependencies
- All 3 specs must be complete and unambiguous
- Strong quality automation (gates, tests)
- Clear decision criteria for kill/continue
- Acceptance that some will fail
